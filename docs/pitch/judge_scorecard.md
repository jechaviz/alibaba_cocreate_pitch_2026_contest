# Judge Scorecard

Goal: make the submission easy to score highly against CoCreate Pitch criteria and hard to dismiss as another generic AI wrapper.

## Winning Frame

Accio Commerce Copilot is not a supplier search tool. It is the buyer decision layer after discovery: a working cockpit that converts supplier options into a defensible buy plan, cash exposure model, risk memo, and next actions.

## Scorecard

| Criterion | What Judges Need To Believe | Demo Proof |
|---|---|---|
| Innovation | We move commerce AI from search to capital allocation. | Live scenario changes margin, cash, risk, and reorder plan. |
| Feasibility | The MVP already models the hard operational math. | V CLI exports catalog, risk, ROI, and evidence JSON. |
| Market potential | Small importers repeatedly face this decision across every SKU. | Unit economics show one avoided bad buy can justify the subscription. |
| Accio Work fit | Accio Work becomes the orchestration surface for sourcing work. | Demo shows brief, RFQ, risk memo, storefront copy, and approval steps. |
| Product readiness | Judges can interact with a real UI and inspect evidence. | Vue demo, screenshots, generated payload, checklist, runbooks. |
| Demo quality | The story resolves in a clear buy/no-buy recommendation. | 90-second path: compare, calculate, de-risk, act. |

## Competitive Objections

| Objection | Rebuttal | Proof To Show |
|---|---|---|
| "Accio already does sourcing." | Great. We are the decision cockpit that turns Accio outputs into a buy plan and launch economics. | Accio workflow + landed-cost panel + risk memo. |
| "Amazon tools already find products." | They validate marketplace demand; they do not fully connect supplier terms, landed cost, MOQ cash exposure, and RFQ execution. | Supplier shortlist and cash-to-first-PO model. |
| "A spreadsheet can do this." | Spreadsheets do math after humans normalize messy inputs. We normalize, score, explain, and generate actions. | Evidence export and agent action trail. |
| "Generic agents can build a plan." | Generic agents lack commerce-specific guardrails, repeatable economics, supplier evidence schema, and audit trail. | Backend deterministic scoring plus visible assumptions. |
| "Sourcing agencies can handle it." | Agencies are high-touch and slow; this is self-serve decision support for smaller teams and early SKU tests. | Fast demo workflow and low SaaS price point. |

## Moat Claims

1. `Decision Graph`: every recommendation links product, supplier, cost, demand, risk, and action state.
2. `Evidence-Led AI`: assumptions stay inspectable, exportable, and usable in a judge/submission packet.
3. `Accio-Native Execution`: Accio Work is not a logo in the deck; it is the workflow surface from brief to RFQ to launch.

## Demo Must-Hit Moments

- Move a price/CAC/freight control and show recommendation pressure immediately.
- Switch supplier and show risk/cash changes, not just a prettier table.
- Click an Accio action and show it creates an operational artifact.
- End on the payload/checklist so judges see submission readiness.
